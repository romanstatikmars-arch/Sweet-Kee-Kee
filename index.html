What is a Business?

A business is when you create something people want or solve a problem for others, and they pay you for it! You can start small and grow big. üå±‚û°Ô∏èüå≥

Step-by-Step Guide to Starting Your First Business

1 Find Your Big Idea!

What are you good at? What do people need? Ideas: lawn mowing, pet sitting, tutoring, making crafts, baking treats, or creating digital art!

2 Make a Plan

Write down: What will you sell? Who will buy it? How much will it cost? Where will you work? A simple plan helps you stay organized!

3 Start Small with Little Money

Use things you already have! Borrow equipment from family. Start with free tools online. Trade your skills with others. Every big business started small!

4 Tell Everyone!

Make flyers, tell neighbors, ask family to spread the word, create social media posts (with parent permission), and be proud of what you're doing!

5 Give Great Service

Be reliable, kind, and do your best work. Happy customers tell their friends, and your business grows!

6 Save & Grow Your Money

Put some money back into your business to make it better. Save some for the future. Spend some on yourself as a reward!

üí° Smart Tip: You don't need lots of money to start! Many successful businesses began with just creativity and hard work. The key is solving a problem for others!

üè† Learning About Property & Homeownership

What Does It Mean to Own Property?

Property means land or buildings that belong to you. When you're older, you can own a home, an apartment building, or land!

Smart Ways to Save for Your Future Home

1 Start Saving Early

Even $5 a week adds up! By the time you're an adult, you'll have a great start. Put birthday money and business profits in a savings account.

2 Learn About "OPM" (Other People's Money)

This means using loans or partnerships. When you're an adult, banks can lend you money to buy property. You pay them back over time. It's like borrowing a big amount now and returning it little by little.

3 Build Good Credit

Credit is like your money report card! When you're 18+, paying bills on time shows you're responsible. Good credit helps you get better loans.

4 Learn About Down Payments

Usually you need to save 3-20% of a home's price. For a $100,000 home, that's $3,000-$20,000. Start saving now, and you'll get there!

5 Explore Special Programs

When you're older, there are programs for first-time homebuyers, veterans, and people with lower incomes. These can help you buy with less money down!

üí° Important: Buying property is for adults with stable income and good planning. Use these years to save, learn, and build good money habits. Your future self will thank you!
